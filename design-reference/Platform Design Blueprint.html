Cadence · Platform design blueprint · v2 · final for sign-off · June 2026

One operator. A staff of agents.
A product loop that never sleeps.

Cadence is an agentic product-management platform: a staff of specialist AI agents (Scout, Strategist, Scribe, Builder, Inspector, Marketer, Quant, Historian) runs the product loop continuously — sensing signals, deciding priorities, building, shipping, learning — while the human operator governs through gates. This document is the platform-level design: what each surface is for, what we are correcting from the current prototype, and what we will modify next — agreed here, before it is implemented in code.

Every screen serves one station of this loop. Anything that doesn't is cut. The gate is the emotional center of the product — the moment the human matters — so gates get the ember color, the strongest motion, and the shortest path from anywhere.

Design principles

Five rules every screen must pass

1 · Calm by default, loud at the gate

The swarm works quietly (parchment, hairlines, small mono labels). Color and motion are reserved for the moments that need a human: gates, failures, budget pressure.

2 · Evidence or it didn't happen

Every agent claim carries a citation chip back to its source (ticket, funnel, call, trace). No naked numbers, no unattributed insights, anywhere.

3 · Consequence-first controls

Buttons say what happens, not what they are: "Approve · opens the PR", "Reject · nothing ships". The cost of a click is always legible before the click.

4 · Editorial, not dashboard

Newsreader serif voice, generous whitespace, one accent. We read like a well-set briefing, not a wall of widgets. Data slop (stat tiles for their own sake) is banned.

5 · The trace is always one click away

Premium trust comes from observability: every AI output links to its full execution trace (model, tokens, cost, hops). Nothing the platform does is a black box — that's the difference between "one among many AI tools" and an enterprise operating system.

Information architecture

Surface map — 7 surfaces, 3 zones

The sidebar is zoned by tempo: Workspace (daily ritual — Today, Approvals, Chat), Loop (the product stations — Product, Missions, Knowledge), and Trust (governance quick-rail + Settings). Anything reachable in the rail is also reachable by ⌘K.

TodayThe hub. Hero brief, Needs-you queue, agent rail, overview tabs. The one screen an operator must see daily.
Approvals (Govern)The engine room, 9 tabs: Controls · Approvals · Guardrails · Budgets · Prompts · Evals · Analytics · Traces · Drift.
ChatConversational command. Goals become missions; the Mission Cockpit embeds inline; every AI reply carries its contract footer.
MissionsThe build station. Live runs, plan steps per specialist, execution traces, cost per mission.
ProductSense + decide, 6 tabs: Signals · Opportunities · Specs · Roadmap · Tasks · Releases — one per loop stage.
KnowledgeThe learn station, 4 tabs: Calendar (default) · Memory · Decisions · Docs. Compounds over time.
SettingsConnectors, model routing, staff toggles, profile. Boring on purpose.
⌘K everywhereSurfaces + missions as first-class destinations. The fastest path between any two points.
Navigation rules
  • Pending gates follow you. Count badges on the nav row and trust rail; the cooking banner names what's running on every screen.
  • State is shared, never copied. Approving a gate in Chat resolves it on Today, in Govern, and on the mission page simultaneously.
  • Three clicks max from any signal to the mission it became, via citation chips and "open mission" links.

Ember stays the only voice that asks for attention. Indigo (orange's complement) carries live state and links; orchid is reserved exclusively for agent actions — tool calls, agent names in traces and cockpit steps — so an operator can scan any screen and instantly see where the machine acted; saffron is for highlights and celebration; moss and madder speak only at outcomes. Cacao and umber replace the gray-leaning espresso/graphite so even the dark surfaces feel warm — but the dark theme itself sits on char, a near-black with only a whisper of warmth: umber bands belong to daylight, and in dark mode the hero band drops below the floor (carved in, not raised) so the theme reads true black, not brown. Status: dark mode is provisionally approved — flagged for a dedicated revamp pass (Phase 3).

Differentiation guardrail: inspiration may come from the best warm-editorial products, but nothing may mimic Anthropic/Claude — no radial flower or asterisk motifs, no coral-on-cream lookalike pairings, no Tiempos/Styrene-adjacent type. Our identity is bilateral (the butterfly), our accent is burnished ember against indigo and orchid, our serif is Newsreader. If a screen could be mistaken for Claude at a glance, it gets redesigned.

Component contracts

The pieces every surface reuses

ComponentContract
Approval cardWho wants what tool, in which mission · evidence summary · expiry · consequence-labeled approve/reject · link to mission. Compact row on Today, detailed card in Govern — same data, two densities.
Mission CockpitTitle + status · numbered specialist steps with live dots · inline gate when one is pending · raw trace toggle · open-mission link. Identical wherever a mission appears.
AI message footerJudge score · model/gateway · latency · tokens · cost · feedback · view-trace · replay-with. Non-negotiable on every AI utterance.
Citation chip[n] inline → hover card with source name + verbatim quote. The atomic unit of trust.
Status dotOne vocabulary platform-wide: indigo pulse = running, ember pulse = needs human, moss = done, madder = failed, faint = planned.
Cooking bannerOne line of what the swarm is doing right now, every screen, dismissible. The heartbeat.
