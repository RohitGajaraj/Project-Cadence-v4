# Positioning and audience

What every post is working toward: position the founder as a top voice in product management who actually ships AI-native product, and pull in the people who matter for the company and the career.

## Positioning statement

A PM-technologist building an agentic Product OS, in public. Most PM voices explain the AI shift. He is shipping it: real frontier-model product, real mechanisms, real decisions, including the ones he got wrong. The brand is "the builder who ships and shows the work," not another commentator.

## The lane (where we win)

The most-followed PM creator, Aakash Gupta, has pivoted almost entirely to AI-PM enablement content and vacated the rigor lanes: classic growth and PLG, metrics rigor, discovery and research craft, and depth over breadth (see [`../references/research-references-aakash-gupta.md`](../references/research-references-aakash-gupta.md)). Two openings follow:

1. **Builder credibility.** He explains the work; we show it being built, with receipts (the bug, the metric, the decision). Depth and proof, not breadth and takes.
2. **The vacated rigor lanes.** Growth, metrics rigor, and discovery craft still have a loyal, under-served audience. We can own them with the authority of someone actually building the tooling.

In one line: explained-by-doers beats explained-by-talkers, and rigor beats hot takes.

## Audience (who we write for)

- **Investors**, especially AI-native ones. Signal they reward: sharp theses, honest metrics, a real moat being built.
- **PM practitioners and product builders.** Signal: usable craft, real lessons, tools that do the work. These are also the product's users.
- **AI-native founders, entrepreneurs, incubators.** Signal: building-in-public candor, a network worth being in.
- **Hiring managers and recruiters** at Google, DeepMind, Anthropic, and YC companies. Signal: demonstrated depth in AI plus PM that is hard to fake.
- **Early adopters** of agentic technology.

Every post should be legible and valuable to at least one of these segments. If it serves none, it does not ship.

## The two goals (every post serves at least one)

1. **Company:** find users for the product, attract investors, and build a real network of founders, operators, and investors.
2. **Career and brand:** become a magnet for top hiring managers and recruiters by demonstrating rare AI-plus-PM depth.

## Non-negotiable: genuine, from real experience

This only works if it is true. Every post is sourced from something that actually happened while building (the [`content-well.md`](./content-well.md)), written in the founder's own evolving voice, and approved by him before it goes out. We do not manufacture takes. We do not post generic AI-written filler. A post that did not come from real work does not get written. The fastest way to sound like automated junk is to automate the thinking, so we automate only the scheduling.
