## X

Most "AI memory" is a write-only log. You store everything, recall almost nothing, and call it a moat.

The number that actually matters: of everything the system saved, what fraction did it later read back?

A store you reopen compounds. A store you don't is a logfile in a trench coat.

## LinkedIn

We shipped a metric this week that I think most AI products quietly avoid.

Everyone counts how much their system "remembers." Stored vectors, a growing graph, a number that photographs well in a deck. The harder number is reads, not writes: of everything you stored, how much did the system actually recall and reuse later?

If that ratio climbs, your product genuinely compounds with use. If it's flat, you have a write-only log wearing a moat costume.

So we put reuse rate on the dashboard, next to growth and "did this learning change a decision." And we left net revenue retention off, with a note saying it needs billing we don't have yet. A blank you can explain beats a number you can't.
