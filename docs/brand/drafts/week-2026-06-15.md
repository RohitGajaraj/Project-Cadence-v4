## Sun 2026-06-21 - Substack: the weekly synthesis (published manually)

- **Publish about 18:24 IST**, teasers Mon 2026-06-22 (X 09:34 IST, LinkedIn 19:02 IST) linking the piece.

**Title:** The AI products that win will not be the smartest

**Body**

One theme kept surfacing for me this week, across the tools I use, a new model launch, and the product problems I find myself thinking about.

Most of the industry is measuring AI on the wrong thing. The whole race is about intelligence: which model scores highest, reasons best, demos cleanest. And the models genuinely are extraordinary. But intelligence is not what makes someone keep using a product. Three moments this week made that concrete.

First, most company AI rollouts are quietly failing, and not because the models are weak. They fail because a tool that dazzles in a demo falls apart when real work, real mess, and real accountability arrive. The missing ingredient is not smarts. It is trust.

Second, the tools you reach for every day have amnesia. They are brilliant in the moment and forget you the instant the session ends. Starting from scratch every time has somehow become normal. The products that break out next will be the ones that actually remember you and grow more useful the longer you stay.

Third, trust is earned by restraint, not by claims. The most honest thing a product can do is leave a number blank when it cannot yet stand behind it. The flashy move is to show more. The trustworthy move is to show only what you can defend. Flash wins the demo. Honesty wins the relationship.

Put it together and the bet is simple. The AI products that win will not be the smartest ones. They will be the ones people trust, that remember them, and that never claim more than they can back up. That is far harder to build than a clever demo. It is also, to me, the only kind worth building, and the kind of problem I find hard to stop thinking about.

More next week, from whatever the work teaches.
