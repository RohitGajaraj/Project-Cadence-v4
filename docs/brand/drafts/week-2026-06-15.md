## Sun 2026-06-21 - Substack: the weekly synthesis (published manually)

- **Publish about 18:24 IST.** Teasers Mon 2026-06-22 (X 09:34 IST, LinkedIn 19:02 IST) linking the piece.

**Title:** The AI products that win will not be the smartest

**Body**

A theme kept surfacing for me this week, across a model I got to use just before it vanished, the tools I lean on daily, and the things I tinker with on the side.

Most of the industry is measuring AI on the wrong thing. The whole race is about intelligence: which model scores highest, reasons best, demos cleanest. And the models genuinely are extraordinary. But intelligence is not what makes someone keep using a product. Three moments this week made that concrete for me.

First, I spent a couple of hours with Fable 5 just before it was pulled, and asked it to design a full design system for an enterprise platform. What struck me was not that it generated components, but that it reasoned about the work: the tradeoffs, the consistency, the questions a good design lead would ask. The shift I felt was from tools that generate to tools that reason.

Second, most company AI rollouts are quietly failing, and not because the models are weak. They fail because a tool that dazzles in a demo falls apart when real work and real accountability arrive. The missing ingredient is not smarts. It is trust.

Third, the tools I reach for every day have amnesia. Brilliant in the moment, and they forget me the instant the session ends. Starting from scratch every time has somehow become normal. The products that break out next will be the ones that actually remember you and grow more useful the longer you stay.

Put it together and the bet I keep landing on is simple. The AI products that win will not be the smartest ones. They will be the ones people trust, that remember them, and that never claim more than they can back up. That is far harder to build than a clever demo. It is also, to me, the only kind worth building, and the problem I find hardest to stop thinking about.
